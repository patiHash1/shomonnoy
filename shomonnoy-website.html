About the Project

Why Shomonnoy exists

Every capstone starts with a real problem. Ours was sitting in plain sight, on the desks of the very students we teach.

Background

Project Shomonnoy was designed and built by a Teach For Bangladesh Fellow as a student-facing capstone, developed together with Grade 6–7 students, teachers, and school leadership to be practical, playful, and genuinely useful.

The Problem

Screen time among 11–14 year-olds has climbed sharply, often crowding out sleep, outdoor play, reading, and face-to-face friendships — with real effects on attention, mood, and physical activity.

Why It Matters

The goal isn't to villainize screens — it's to help students build a healthy, self-aware relationship with technology, so it supports their curiosity and creativity instead of replacing it.

Vision & Mission

What we're building toward

Vision

A generation that chooses balance, not restriction.

We imagine students who use technology with intention — confident enough to put the phone down and pick up a paintbrush, a football, or a friend.

Mission

Equip students with habits, not just warnings.

Through workshops, challenges, and peer clubs, we give students practical tools to notice their screen habits and replace them with things they genuinely enjoy.

Curiosity

Redirecting screen time energy into asking bigger questions.

Balance

Not zero screens — the right amount, at the right time.

Community

Habits stick best when built alongside friends, not alone.

Creativity

Offline hobbies as the reward, not homework in disguise.
